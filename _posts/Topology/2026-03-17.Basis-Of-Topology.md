## Definition
Let $X$ be a set. A ***basis*** for a topology on $X$ is a collection $\mathcal{B}$ of subsets of $X$ such that

**(i)** For each $x \in X$, there exists $B \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x \in B$.

**(ii)** If $x \in B_1 \cap B_2$ for $B_1, B_2 \in \mathcal{B}$, then there exists $B_3 \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x \in B_3 \subseteq B_1 \cap B_2$.

If $\mathcal{B}$ satisfies these two conditions, then we define the ***topology $\mathcal{T}$ generated by $\mathcal{B}$*** as follows: A subset $U$ of $X$ is said to be ***open*** in $X$ (that is, to be an element of $\mathcal{T}$) if for each $x \in U$, there exists $B \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x \in B \subseteq U$. Note that each basis element is itself an element of $\mathcal{T}$. Then the collection $\mathcal{T}$ is a topology on $X$.
