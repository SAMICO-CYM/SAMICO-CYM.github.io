## Definition
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space. 

**(i)** A ***subbasis*** for $\mathcal{T}$ is a collection $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $X$ such that 

$$\bigcap_{S \in \mathcal{S}} S = X.$$

---

## Theorem 1
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space, and let $\mathcal{S}$ be a subbasis for $\mathcal{T}$.

(i) Define the collection

$$\mathcal{B} _ {\mathcal{S}} = \left\{ \bigcap_{S \in \mathcal{S}'} S \,\, \Big \vert \,\, \mathcal{S}' \subset \mathcal{S}, \vert \mathcal{S}' \vert < \infty \right\}$$


**(ii)** The ***topology generated by the subbasis*** $\mathcal{S}$ is defined to be the collection of all unions of finite intersections of members of $\mathcal{S}$. 

---

## Theorem
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be a subbasis for a topology on $X$. Then $\mathcal{T} _{\mathcal{S}}$ is the smallest topology on $X$ that contains $\mathcal{S}$.
