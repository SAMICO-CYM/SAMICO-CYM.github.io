## Definition
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space. A ***subbasis*** for $\mathcal{T}$ is a collection $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $X$ such that 

$$\bigcap_{S \in \mathcal{S}} S = X.$$

---

## Theorem 1
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space, and let $\mathcal{S}$ be a subbasis for $\mathcal{T}$. Then the collection $\mathcal{B} _ {\mathcal{S}}$ defined by

$$\mathcal{B} _ {\mathcal{S}} = \left\{ \bigcap_{S \in \mathcal{S}'} S \,\, \Bigg \vert \,\, \mathcal{S}' \subset \mathcal{S}, \vert \mathcal{S}' \vert < \infty \right\}$$

is a basis for $\mathcal{T}$. Then the topology $\mathcal{T}$ is generated by $\mathcal{B} _ {\mathcal{S}}$. In this case, we say that $\mathcal{S}$ is a ***subbasis for the topology*** $\mathcal{T}$, or $\mathcal{T}$ is ***generated by*** $\mathcal{S}$.

---

## Theorem
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be a subbasis for a topology on $X$. Then $\mathcal{T} _{\mathcal{S}}$ is the smallest topology on $X$ that contains $\mathcal{S}$.
