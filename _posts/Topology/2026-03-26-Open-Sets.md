## Definition
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space. If a subset $A$ of $X$ is an element of $\mathcal{T}$, then $A$ is called an ***open set*** of $X$, or $A$ is said to be open in $X$.

---

## Theorem
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space, and $\mathcal{B}$ be a basis for $X$. Let $A \subset X$. TFAE.

**(i)** $A$ is open in $X$.

**(ii)** $x \in A \implies \exists U \in \mathcal{T}$ such that $x \in U \subset A$.

**(iii)** $\displaystyle A = \bigcup U_{\alpha}$ where $\\{ U_{\alpha} \\} \subset \mathcal{T}$.

**(iv)** $x \in A \implies \exists B \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x \in B \subset A$.

### Proof
**(i)** $\iff$ **(iv)**

By definition of $\mathcal{T} _{\mathcal{B}}$, it is clear.

**(i)** $\implies$ **(ii)**

Suppose that $A$ is open in $X$, i.e., $A \in \mathcal{T}$. If $x \in A$, we let $U = A$. Then clearly **(ii)** holds.

**(ii)** $\implies$ **(i)**

If $A = \emptyset$, then clearly $A \in \mathcal{T}$, so that $A$ is open in $X$.

Suppose that $A \neq \emptyset$. We show that

$$A = \bigcup_{x \in A} U_x$$

where $U_x \in \mathcal{T}$ and $x \in U_x \subset A$. Let $x \in A$. Then $x \in U_x \subset \bigcup_{x \in A} U_x$. 

Conversely, if $x \in \bigcup_{x \in A} U_x$, then $x \in U_y$ for some $y \in A$. Since $U_y \subset A$, we have $x \in A$. Thus $A = \bigcup_{x \in A} U_x$.

**(ii)** $\implies$ **(iii)**
