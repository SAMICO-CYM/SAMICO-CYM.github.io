## Definition
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space. If a subset $A$ of $X$ is an element of $\mathcal{T}$, then $A$ is called an ***open set*** of $X$, or $A$ is said to be ***open*** in $X$.

---

## Theorem
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a topological space, and $\mathcal{B}$ be a basis for $\mathcal{T}$. Let $A \subset X$. TFAE.

**(i)** $A$ is open in $X$.

**(ii)** $x \in A \implies \exists U \in \mathcal{T}$ such that $x \in U \subset A$.

**(iii)** $\displaystyle A = \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$ where $\mathcal{A} \subset \mathcal{T}$.

**(iv)** $x \in A \implies \exists B \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x \in B \subset A$.

### Proof
**(i)** $\iff$ **(iv)**

By definition of $\mathcal{T} _ {\mathcal{B}}$, it is clear.

**(i)** $\implies$ **(ii)**

Suppose that $A$ is open in $X$, i.e., $A \in \mathcal{T}$. If $x \in A$, we let $U = A$. Then clearly **(ii)** holds.

**(ii)** $\implies$ **(i)**

If $A = \emptyset$, then clearly $A \in \mathcal{T}$, so that $A$ is open in $X$.

Suppose that $A \neq \emptyset$. Let $\mathcal{A} = \\{ U \in \mathcal{T} \mid U \subset A \\}$. We show that

$$
A = \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$$

Let $x \in A$. By assumption, $\exists U \in \mathcal{T}$ such that $x \in U \subset A$, which means that $U \in \mathcal{A}$. Then $x \in U \subset \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$. 

Let $x \in \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$. Then $x \in U$ for some $U \in \mathcal{A}$. Since $U \in \mathcal{A}$, $U \subset A$. Thus $x \in A$. Therefore $A = \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$.

Since $\mathcal{T}$ is a topology, $\bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U \in \mathcal{T}$, so that $A \in \mathcal{T}$. Thus $A$ is open in $X$.

**(ii)** $\implies$ **(iii)**

We have shown that $A = \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$ where $\mathcal{A} \subset \mathcal{T}$. Thus **(iii)** holds.

**(iii)** $\implies$ **(ii)**

Suppose that $A = \bigcup_{U \in \mathcal{A}} U$ where $\mathcal{A} \subset \mathcal{T}$. If $x \in A$, then $x \in U$ for some $U \in \mathcal{A}$. Since each $U$ is an open set and $U \subset A$, we have $x \in U \subset A$. Thus **(ii)** holds. $\blacksquare$
