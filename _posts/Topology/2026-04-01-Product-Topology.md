## Theorem 1
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{T}')$ be topological spaces. 

**(i)** The collection 

$$\mathcal{B} = \{ U \times V \mid U \in \mathcal{T}, V \in \mathcal{T}' \}$$

of subsets of $X \times Y$ is a basis on $X \times Y$. The topology generated by $\mathcal{B}$ is called the ***product topology*** on $X \times Y$.

**(ii)** If $\mathcal{B}$ is a basis for $\mathcal{T}$ and $\mathcal{C}$ is a basis for $\mathcal{T}'$, then 

$$\mathcal{D} = \{ B \times C \mid B \in \mathcal{B}, C \in \mathcal{C} \}$$

is also a basis for the product topology on $X \times Y$.

## Remark
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{T}')$ be topological spaces, and let $\pi_1: X \times Y \to X$ and $\pi_2: X \times Y \to Y$ be the projection maps. If $U \in \mathcal{T}$ and $V \in \mathcal{T}'$, then we have 

$$\begin{align*}
\pi_1^{-1}(U) &= U \times Y \\
\pi_2^{-1}(V) &= X \times V,
\end{align*}$$

so that 

$$\pi_1^{-1}(U) \cap \pi_2^{-1}(V) = (U \times Y) \cap (X \times V) = U \times V.$$

## Theorem 2
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{T}')$ be topological spaces. The collection

$$\mathcal{S} = \{ \pi_1^{-1}(U) \mid U \in \mathcal{T} \} \cup \{ \pi_2^{-1}(V) \mid V \in \mathcal{T}' \}$$

is a basis for the product topology on $X \times Y$.
