## Definition
A ***topology*** on a set $X$ is a collection $\mathcal{T} \subset 2^X$ such that

**(i)** $\emptyset, X \in \mathcal{T}$

**(ii)** The union of the elements of any subcollection of $\mathcal{T}$ is in $\mathcal{T}$. That is, 

$$\bigcup_{\alpha \in A \subset \mathcal{T}} U_\alpha \in \mathcal{T}$$

**(iii)** The intersection of the elements of any finite subcollection of $\mathcal{T}$ is in $\mathcal{T}$. That is, 

$$\bigcap_{i=1}^n U_i \in \mathcal{T}$$

An ordered pair $(X, \mathcal{T})$ is called a ***topological space***, and each element of $\mathcal{T}$ is called a ***open set*** of $X$.

정의를 보면 알겠지만 open set의 성질을 모두 가져다 놓고 이를 만족하는 각 원소들을 모아놓은 집합을 topology라고 정의한다.

---

## Example
Let $X$ be a set. 

**(i)** The topology $\mathcal{T} = \\{ \emptyset, X \\}$ on $X$ is called the ***trivial topology***.

**(ii)** The topology $\mathcal{T} = 2^X$ on $X$ is called the ***discrete topology***.

**(iii)** The collection $\mathcal{T}_f = \\{ U \subset X \mid X \setminus U \text{ is either finite or } X \\}$ of subsets of $X$ is called the ***cofinite topology*** or the ***finite complement topology***. 

**(iv)** The collection $\mathcal{T}_c = \\{ U \subset X \mid X \setminus U \text{ is either countable or } X \\}$ of subsets of $X$ is called the ***cocountable topology*** or the ***countable complement topology***. 

$(\because)$ Since $X \setminus \emptyset = X$ and $X \setminus X = \emptyset$ is finite, we have $\emptyset, X \in \mathcal{T}_f$. Let $\\{U_\alpha \\}$ be a subcollection of $\mathcal{T}_f$. Then we have

$$\begin{align*}
X \setminus \bigcup_\alpha U_\alpha &= X \cap \left( \bigcup_\alpha U_\alpha \right)^c \\
&= X \cap \bigcap_\alpha U^c_\alpha \\
&= \bigcap_\alpha (X \cap U^c_\alpha) \\
&= \bigcap_\alpha (X \setminus U_\alpha).
\end{align*}$$

Since each $U_\alpha$ is an element of $\mathcal{T}_f$, each $X \setminus U_\alpha$ is either finite or $X$. Thus the intersection is also either finite or $X$, which means that the union belongs to $\mathcal{T}_f$.

Let $\\{ U_1, ..., U_n \\}$ be a finite subcollection of $\mathcal{T}_f$. Then we have 

$$\begin{align*}
X \setminus \bigcap_{i=1}^n U_i &= X \cap \left( \bigcap_{i=1}^n U_i \right)^c \\
&= X \cap \bigcup_{i=1}^n U^c_i \\
&= \bigcup_{i=1}^n (X \cap U^c_i) \\
&= \bigcup_{i=1}^n (X \setminus U_i).
\end{align*}$$

Since each $X \setminus U_i$ is either finite or $X$, the union is also either finite or $X$. Thus the finite intersection is an element of $\mathcal{T}_f$, and therefore the collection $\mathcal{T}_f$ is a topology on $X$. $\blacksquare$

Cofinite topology $(X, \mathcal{T}_f)$의 원소가 어떤 꼴인지 살펴보자. 정의상 $\emptyset$과 $X$는 들어가 있어야 하고, 그 외에 어떤 집합이 이 위상의 원소가 될 수 있는지 결정해야 한다. 이러한 집합을 전체 공간 $X$에서 빼면 유한한 원소만이 남아있어야 하고, $A \backslash (A \backslash B) = B$가 성립하기 때문에 $X$의 임의의 유한 부분집합 $\\{ x_1, ..., x_n \\}$에 대하여 $U = X \backslash \\{ x_1, ..., x_n \\}$ 꼴의 집합은 모두 이 위상의 원소가 된다.
