## Definition
Let $V$ be a real vector space, and let $X \subset V$.

**(i)** $X$ is said to be ***convex*** if 

$$t x + (1 - t)y \in X, \quad \forall x, y \in X, \forall t \in [0, 1].$$

**(ii)** Let $X$ be convex and let $f : X \to \mathbb{R}$. Then $f$ is called ***convex*** if 

$$f(tx + (1-t)y) \le tf(x) + (1-t)f(y), \quad \forall x, y \in X, \forall t \in [0, 1].$$
