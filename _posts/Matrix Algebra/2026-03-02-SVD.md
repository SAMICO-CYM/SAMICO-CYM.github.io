## Lemma 1
Let $A \in \mathbb{C}^{m \times n}$. Then the followings hold:
1. The matrix $A^* A \in \mathbb{C}^{n \times n}$ is Hermitian and positive-semidefinite, i.e., it has $n$ nonnegative real eigenvalues, say 

$$\sigma_1 \ge \sigma_2 \ge \cdots \ge \sigma_r > 0, \, \, \sigma_{r+1} = \cdots = \sigma_n = 0$$

belonging to eigenvectors 

$$\mathbf{v_1}, \mathbf{v_2}, \cdots, \mathbf{v_r}, \mathbf{v_{r+1}}, \cdots, \mathbf{v_n},$$

respectively, which are chosen to be orthonormal. Then the eigenvectors $\mathbf{v_1}, \mathbf{v_2}, \cdots, \mathbf{v_r}$ of $A^*A$ belonging to positive eigenvalues form an orthonormal basis for the row space $\mathcal{R}(A) = \mathcal{R}(A^{*}A)$ in $\mathbb{C}^n$, where $\operatorname{rank}(A) = r$, and the eigenvectors $\mathbf{v_{r+1}}, \cdots, \mathbf{v_n}$ belonging to zero eigenvalue form an orthonormal basis for the null space $\mathcal{N}(A)$.
2. For positive $\sigma_i, i = 1, 2, ..., r$, set 

$$\mathbf{u_i} = \frac{A\mathbf{v_i}}{\sqrt{\sigma_i}}.$$

Then the vectors $\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, ..., \mathbf{u}_r$ form an orthonormal basis for the column space $\mathcal{C}(A)( = \mathcal{R}(A^{*}) = \mathcal{R}(AA^{*}))$. Hence 

$$\sqrt{\sigma_i} = \| A \mathbf{v}_i \|.$$

3. The unit vectors $\mathbf{v}_i \in \mathcal{R}(A)$ and $\mathbf{u}_i \in \mathcal{C}(A)$ are related by the following equations: 

$$A \mathbf{v}_i = \sqrt{\sigma_i} \mathbf{u}_i \quad \text{and} \quad A^*\mathbf{u}_i = \sqrt{\sigma_i} \mathbf{v}_i, \quad i = 1, 2, ..., r.$$

Thus the vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, ..., \mathbf{v}_r$ are the eigenvectors of $A^*A$ belonging to positive eigenvalues $\sigma_1, \sigma_2, ..., \sigma_r$. Similarly, the vectors $\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, ..., \mathbf{u}_r$ are the eigenvectors of $AA^*$ belonging to positive eigenvalues $\sigma_1, \sigma_2, ..., \sigma_r$.

### Proof
1. Since $(A^*A)^* = A^*A$, $A^*A$ is Hermitian. For $x \in \mathbb{C}^n$, 

$$x^*A^*Ax = (Ax)^*Ax = \langle Ax, Ax \rangle = \| Ax \|^2 \ge 0,$$

which means that $A^*A$ is positive-semidefinite. Thus $A^*A$ is unitarily diagonalizable, so that it has $n$ nonnegative real eigenvalues

## Definition 1
- Using the notation of the above lemma, the square roots $\sqrt{\sigma_1}, \sqrt{\sigma_2}, ..., \sqrt{\sigma_r}$ of positive eigenvalues of $A^*A$ are called ***singular values*** of a matrix $A$. 
- The basis vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, ..., \mathbf{v}_r$ for $\mathcal{R}(A)$ are called the ***right singular vectors*** of $A$. Similarly, basis vectors $\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, ..., \mathbf{u}_r$ for $\mathcal{C}(A)$ are called the ***left singular vectors*** of $A$.

## Singular Value Decomposition
- Let $A \in \mathbb{C}^{m \times n}$ with singular values $\sqrt{\sigma}_1 \ge \sqrt{\sigma}_2 \ge \cdots \ge \sqrt{\sigma}_r$ in descending order, and corresponding right singular vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, ..., \mathbf{v}_r$, and left singular vectors $\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, ..., \mathbf{u}_r$. Then the ***singular value decomposition*** of $A$ is given by 

$$A = U \Sigma V^* = \sum_{i=1}^r \sqrt{\sigma_i} \mathbf{u}_i \mathbf{v}_i^*$$
