## Definition
Let $R$ be a relation on a set $X$. 

**(i)** If $R$ is reflexive, antisymmetric, and transitive, then $R$ is called a ***partial order***, and a pair $(X, R)$ is called a ***partially ordered set*** (or ***poset***).

**(ii)** A partial order $R$ on $X$ such that $\forall x, y \in X, xRy \vee yRx$ is called a ***total order*** (or a ***linear order***), and a pair $(X, R)$ is called a ***totally ordered set*** (or ***linearly ordered set***).

**(iii)** A totally ordered subset of a poset is called a ***chain***.
