## Definition
**(i)** A ***relation*** $R$ from $A$ to $B$ is a subset of $A \times B$. We denote $(a, b) \in R$ by $aRb$.

**(ii)** Let $A$ and $B$ be sets, and let $R$ be a relation from $A$ to $B$. Then ***inverse*** $R^{-1}$ of $R$ is the relation from $B$ to $A$ such that $bR^{-1}a \iff aRb$. That is, $R^{-1} = \\{(b,a) \mid aRb \\}$.

**(iii)** Let $R$ be a relation from $A$ to $B$. Then the ***domain*** of $R$, denoted by $\operatorname{Dom}(R)$, is the set 

$$\operatorname{Dom}(R) = \{ a \in A \mid \exists b \in B (aRb) \}$$

The ***image*** of $R$, denoted by $\operatorname{Im}(R)$, is the set 

$$\operatorname{Im}(R) = \{ b \in B \mid \exists a \in A (aRb) \}$$

## Remark
Let $A$ and $B$ be sets, and let $R$ be a relation from $A$ to $B$. 

**(i)** $\operatorname{Dom}(R) = \operatorname{Im}(R^{-1})$ and $\operatorname{Im}(R) = \operatorname{Dom}(R^{-1})$.

**(ii)** If $A = B$, then the relation $R$ is called the relation ***in*** $A$. In this case, if $\operatorname{Dom}(R) = A$, then $R$ is called the relation ***on*** $A$
