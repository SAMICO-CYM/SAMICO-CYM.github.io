## Definition 1
Let $A$ and $B$ be sets.

**(i)** A ***relation*** $R$ from $A$ to $B$ is a subset of $A \times B$. We denote $(a, b) \in R$ by $aRb$.

**(ii)** Let $R$ be a relation from $A$ to $B$. Then ***inverse*** $R^{-1}$ of $R$ is the relation from $B$ to $A$ such that $bR^{-1}a \iff aRb$. That is, $R^{-1} = \\{(b,a) \mid aRb \\}$.

**(iii)** Let $R$ be a relation from $A$ to $B$. Then the ***domain*** of $R$, denoted by $\operatorname{Dom}(R)$, is the set 

$$\operatorname{Dom}(R) = \{ a \in A \mid \exists b \in B (aRb) \}$$

The ***image*** of $R$, denoted by $\operatorname{Im}(R)$, is the set 

$$\operatorname{Im}(R) = \{ b \in B \mid \exists a \in A (aRb) \}$$

**(iv)** If $R$ is a relation on $X$ and $Y \subset X$, then the relation

$$R \vert_Y = \{ (x, y) \in R \mid x, y \in Y \} = R \cap (Y \times Y)$$

is called the ***restriction*** of $R$ to $Y$.

## Example
실수 공간 $\mathbb{R}$위에서 정의된 관계 $\le$에 대해서 $\le^{-1} \,\, = \,\, \ge$이다. 또한 $\le \backslash \triangle_\mathbb{R} = <$이다. 

## Remark 1
Let $A$ and $B$ be sets, and let $R$ be a relation from $A$ to $B$. 

**(i)** $\operatorname{Dom}(R) = \operatorname{Im}(R^{-1})$ and $\operatorname{Im}(R) = \operatorname{Dom}(R^{-1})$.

**(ii)** If $A = B$, then the relation $R$ is called the relation ***in*** $A$. In this case, if $\operatorname{Dom}(R) = A$, then $R$ is called the relation ***on*** $A$

## Definition 2
Let $A, B$ and $C$ be sets, and let $R \subset A \times B$ and $S \subset B \times C$ be relations. Then we define the ***composite of relations***, denoted by $R \circ S$, by

$$R \circ S = \{ (a, c) \in A \times C \mid \exists b \in B \, [(a, b) \in R \wedge (b, c) \in S ] \}$$

## Remark 2
함수의 합성과 관계의 합성은 적는 순서가 반대임을 유의하자. 함수 $f: X \to Y$와 $g:Y \to Z$의 합성은 $g \circ f:X \to Z$로, 먼저 매핑되는 함수를 오른쪽에 적는 반면, 관계 $R \subset A \times B$와 $S \subset B \times C$의 합성은 그냥 순서 그대로 $R \circ S$로 적게 된다.

## Definition 3
Let $R$ be a relation in a set $X$. Then we say that 

**(i)** $R$ is ***reflexive*** $\iff$ $xRx, \forall x \in X$.

**(ii)** $R$ is ***symmetric*** $\iff$ $xRy \Longrightarrow yRx, \forall x, y \in X$.

**(iii)** $R$ is ***transitive*** $\iff$ $xRy \wedge yRz \Longrightarrow xRz, \forall x, y, z \in X$. 

**(iv)** $R$ is ***antisymmetric*** $\iff$ $xRy \wedge yRx \implies x = y$.

## Theorem 1
Let $R$ be a relation on $X$. 

**(i)** $R$ is reflexive $\iff$ $\triangle_X \subset R$

**(ii)** $R$ is symmetric $\iff$ $R = R^{-1}$

**(iii)** $R$ is antisymmetric $\iff$ $R \cap R^{-1} \subset\triangle_X$

**(iv)** $R$ is transitive $\iff$ $R \circ R \subset R$

### Proof
$\blacksquare$
