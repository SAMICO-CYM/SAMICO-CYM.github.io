## Definition 1
Let $(X, \le)$ be a poset. A pair $(x, y)$ is called a ***cover*** if 

**(i)** $x \le y$

**(ii)** $\forall z \in X (x \le z \le y \Longrightarrow z = x \vee z = y)$.

We often say that $x$ is covered by $y$ or $y$ covers $x$, and denote it by $x \lhd y$.

## Example
Poset $(\mathbb{N}, \le)$이 주어져 있으면 $(1, 2)$는 커버지만 $(1, 3)$은 커버가 아니다. 즉 커버는 일종의 가장 가까운 관계 내지는 관계의 최소 단위를 의미한다고 직관적으로 이해할 수 있다. 

## Definition 2
The ***Hasse diagram*** of a poset $(X, \le)$ is a drawing of the elements and the covers of $X$ such that if $x \lhd y$, then $x$ is below $y$. 

## Theorem
Let $(X, \le)$ be a finite poset, and let $x, y \in X$. Then $x \le y$ $\iff$ $\exists x_1, ..., x_n \in X$ such that 

$$x = x_1 \lhd x_2 \lhd \cdots \lhd x_n = y.$$

### Proof
$(\Longrightarrow)$

Suppose that $x \le y$. Let $n$ be the maximum integer for which $a = x_1 \lhd \cdots \lhd x_n = y$ for some distinct elements $x_1, ..., x_n$ in $X$. (Such integer $n$ exists as $a \le b$ gives $x = x_1 \lhd x_2 = y$ and $X$ is finite.) 

Note that each $x_i$ is covered by $x_{i+1}$ because if not, then we could extend our sequence $x_1 \lhd \cdots \lhd x_n$ to $x_1 \lhd \cdots \lhd x_n \lhd x_{n+1}$, which contradicts the maximality of $n$. Thus $x_i \lhd x_{i+1}$ for all $i \in \\{1, ..., n-1\\}$.

$(\Longleftarrow)$

Assume that $\exists x_1, ..., x_n \in X$ such that 

$$x = x_1 \lhd x_2 \lhd \cdots \lhd x_n = y.$$

Since $x_i \lhd x_{i+1}, \forall i \in \\{1, ..., n-1\\}$ and $\le$ is transitive, we have $x \le y$. $\blacksquare$
