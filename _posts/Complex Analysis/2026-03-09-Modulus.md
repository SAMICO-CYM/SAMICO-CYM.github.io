## Definition
Let $z = x + iy$ be a complex number. The ***modulus*** or ***absolute value*** of $z$, denoted by $\vert z \vert$, is defined by

$$\vert z \vert = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}.$$

주의할 점은 여기서 $x, y$는 $\mathbb{R} \times \\{ 0 \\}$에 mapping된, 즉 complex number로 identified된 숫자가 아니라 그 자체로 실수 공간 $\mathbb{R}$의 원소이다. 즉 

$$\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \neq \sqrt{(x,0)^2 + (y,0)^2}$$

으로 정의되는 것이 아님을 주의하자. 

한편 위와 같이 정의되는 modulus는 자명하게 놈의 정의를 만족하고, 자연스럽게 복소 공간 $\mathbb{C}$에 거리 $d(z_1, z_2) = \vert z_1 - z_2 \vert$를 부여할 수 있다. 이로써 극한의 개념을 논할 수 있게 된다.

---

## Remark
Let $z = x + iy$ be a complex number. Then 

$$\text{Re} z \le \vert \text{Re} z \vert \le \vert z \vert \quad \text{and} \quad \text{Im} z \le \vert \text{Im} z \vert \le \vert z \vert.$$

## Theorem
Let $z_1, z_2 \in \mathbb{C}$. Then

**(i)** $\vert z_1 z_2 \vert = \vert z_1 \vert \vert z_2 \vert$

**(ii)** $\vert z_1 + z_2 \vert \le \vert z_1 \vert + \vert z_2 \vert$

**(iii)** $\vert z_1 - z_2 \vert \ge \vert \vert z_1 \vert - \vert z_2 \vert \vert$

### Proof
Let $z_1 = (x_1, y_1)$ and $z_2 = (x_2, y_2)$.

**(i)** Since $z_1z_2 = (x_1x_2 - y_1y_2, x_1y_2 + x_2y_1)$, we have 

$$\begin{align*} 
\vert z_1z_2 \vert^2 &= (x_1x_2 - y_1y_2)^2 + (x_1y_2 + x_2y_1)^2 \\ 
&= (x_1x_2)^2 - 2x_1x_2y_1y_2 + (y_1y_2)^2 + (x_1y_2)^2 + 2x_1x_2y_1y_2 + (x_2y_1)^2 \\ 
&= (x_1x_2)^2 + (y_1y_2)^2 + (x_1y_2)^2 + (x_2y_1)^2 \\ 
&= (x_1^2 + y_1^2)(x_2^2 + y_2^2) \\ 
&= \vert z_1 \vert^2 \vert z_2 \vert^2
\end{align*}$$

Taking the square root of both sides, we get $\vert z_1z_2 \vert = \vert z_1 \vert \vert z_2 \vert$. 

**(ii)** Note that 

$$\begin{align*}
\vert z_1 + z_2 \vert^2 & = (z_1 + z_2) \overline{z_1+z_2} \\
& = (z_1 + z_2) (\overline{z_1} + \overline{z_2}) \\
& = z_1\overline{z_1} + z_1\overline{z_2} + z_2\overline{z_1} + z_2\overline{z_2} \\
& = \vert z_1 \vert^2 + \vert z_2 \vert^2 + z_1\overline{z_2} + \overline{z_1\overline{z_2}} \\
& = \vert z_1 \vert^2 + \vert z_2 \vert^2 + 2\text{Re}(z_1\overline{z_2}) \\
& \le \vert z_1 \vert^2 + \vert z_2 \vert^2 + 2\vert z_1\overline{z_2} \vert \\
& = \vert z_1 \vert^2 + \vert z_2 \vert^2 + 2\vert z_1 \vert \vert z_2 \vert \\
& = (\vert z_1 \vert + \vert z_2 \vert)^2
\end{align*}$$

Taking the square root of both sides, we get $\vert z_1 + z_2 \vert \le \vert z_1 \vert + \vert z_2 \vert$.

**(iii)**  Note that 

$$\begin{align*}
\vert z_1 \vert &= \vert (z_1 + z_2) - z_2 \vert \\
& \le \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert + \vert -z_2 \vert \\
& = \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert + \vert z_2 \\
& \implies \vert z_1 \vert - \vert z_2 \vert \le \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert
\end{align*}$$

Similarly, we have 

$$\begin{align*}
\vert z_2 \vert &= \vert (z_1 + z_2) - z_1 \vert \\
& \le \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert + \vert -z_1 \vert \\
& = \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert + \vert z_1 \\
& \implies \vert z_2 \vert - \vert z_1 \vert \le \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert
\end{align*}$$

Combining the two inequalities, we get 

$$\begin{align*}
\vert z_1 \vert - \vert z_2 \vert \le \vert z_1 + z_2 \vert \le \vert z_1 \vert + \vert z_2 \\
& \implies \vert z_1 - z_2 \vert \ge \vert \vert z_1 \vert - \vert z_2 \vert \vert
\end{align*}$$

$\blacksquare$

## Example
Let $n \in \mathbb{N}$ and let $a_0, a_1, ..., a_n \in \mathbb{C}$ where $a_n \neq 0$. Let 

$$P(z) = a_0 + a_1z+ \cdots + a_nz^n$$

be a polynomial of degree $n$. Then there exists $R > 0$ such that 

$$\left \vert \frac{1}{P(z)} \right \vert < \frac{2}{\vert a_n \vert R^n} \quad \text{whenever} \quad \vert z \vert > R.$$

$(\because)$ Let 

$$w = \frac{a_0}{z^n} + \frac{a_1}{z^{n-1}} + \cdots + \frac{a_{n-1}}{z^1} \quad (z \neq 0)$$

so that 

$$P(z) = (w + a_n)z^n$$

when $z \neq 0$. Then we have 

$$\begin{gather*}
wz^n = a_1 + a_1z + \cdots + a_{n-1}z^{n-1} \\
\implies \vert w \vert \vert z \vert^n \le \vert a_0 \vert + \vert a_1 \vert \vert z \vert + \cdots + \vert a_{n-1} \vert \vert z \vert^{n-1} \\
\vert w \vert \le \frac{\vert a_0 \vert}{\vert z \vert^n} + \frac{\vert a_1 \vert}{\vert z \vert^{n-1}} + \cdots + \frac{\vert a_{n-1} \vert}{\vert z \vert} \quad (z \neq 0)
\end{gather*}$$

We claim that $\exists R > 0$ such that for any $k \in \\{ 1, 2, ..., n \\}$, 

$$\frac{|a_{n-k}|}{|z|^k} < \frac{|a_n|}{2n} \quad \text{for } \vert z \vert > R.$$

$(\because)$ Note that for each $k = 1, 2, \dots, n$, $\frac{\vert a_{n-k} \vert}{\vert a_n \vert} 2n$ is some fixed nonnegative real number. Choose $R > 1$ such that 

$$\max_{1 \le k \le n} \left\{ \frac{|a_{n-k}|}{|a_n|} \cdot 2n \right\} \le R.$$ 

For any $k \in \{1, 2, \dots, n\}$, we have 

$$ \begin{gather*} 
\frac{|a_{n-k}|}{|a_n|} \cdot 2n \le R \le R^k < |z|^k \quad \text{for } |z| > R \\
\implies \frac{|a_{n-k}|}{|z|^k} < \frac{|a_n|}{2n} \quad \text{for } |z| > R. 
 \end{gather*}$$

Thus

$$\begin{align*}
& \vert w \vert < n \cdot \frac{\vert a_n \vert}{2n} = \frac{\vert a_n \vert}{2} \quad \text{for } \vert z \vert > R \\
\implies &\vert a_n + w \vert \ge \vert \vert a_n \vert - \vert w \vert \vert = \left\vert \frac{\vert a_n \vert}{2} + \frac{\vert a_n \vert}{2} - \vert w \vert \right\vert \ge \frac{\vert a_n \vert}{2} \quad \text{for } \vert z \vert > R \\
\implies &\vert P(z) \vert = \vert w + a_n \vert \vert z \vert^n > \frac{\vert a_n \vert}{2} R^n \quad \text{for } \vert z \vert > R \\ 
\implies &\left \vert \frac{1}{P(z)} \right \vert < \frac{2}{\vert a_n \vert R^n} \quad \text{for } \vert z \vert > R. \blacksquare
\end{align*}$$
