## Definition
An ***abelian group*** $(G, \ast)$ is a group such that $\ast$ is commutative.

## Examples

**(i)** $(\mathbb{Z}, +), (\mathbb{Q}, +)$, and $(\mathbb{R}, +)$ are abelian groups.

**(ii)** $(\mathbb{Q}^{\ast}, \cdot)$ and $(\mathbb{R}^{\ast}, \cdot)$ are abelian groups.

**(iii)** $(\mathbb{R}^{m \times n}, +)$ is an abelian group.

**(iv)** $(GL(n, \mathbb{R}), \cdot)$ and $(SL(n, \mathbb{R}), \cdot)$ are not abelian groups.
