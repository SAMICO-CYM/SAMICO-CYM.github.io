## Definition
**(i)** Let $(S, \ast)$ and $(T, \ast')$ be binary algebraic structures. Then an ***isomorphism*** of $S$ with $T$ is a bijection $\phi : S \to T$ such that 

$$\phi(x \ast y) = \phi(x) \ast' \phi(y) \forall x, y \in S$$

**(ii)** If such a map $\phi$ exists, then $S$ and $T$ are said to be ***isomorphic*** binary structures and we denote by $S \cong T$ (or $S \simeq T$).
