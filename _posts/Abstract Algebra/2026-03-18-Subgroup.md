## Definition
**(i)** Let $H$ be a nonempty subset of a group $G$ which is closed under the binary operation on $G$. Then $H$ is called a ***subgroup*** of $G$ if $H$ is a group under the induced binary operation from $G$. In this case, we write $H \leq G$. 

**(ii)** Let $H \le G$. Then $H$ is called a ***proper subgroup*** of $G$ if $H \ne G$. In this case, we write $H \subsetneq G$.

**(iii)** Let $H \le G$. Then $H$ is called the ***trivial subgroup*** of $G$ if $H = \\{ e \\}$, where $e$ is the identity element of $G$.

---

## Example
**(i)** Let $A = \\{ (a_1, ..., a_{n-1}, 0) \mid a_i \in \mathbb{R} \\}$. Then $A < \mathbb{R}^n$.

**(ii)** $(\mathbb{Q}^+, \cdot) < (\mathbb{R}^+, \cdot)$

**(iii)** Let $U_n = \\{ z \in \mathbb{C} \mid z^n = 1 \\}$. Then $(U_n, \cdot)$ is a group and is called ***the group of $n$th roots of unity***, and we have $(U_n, \cdot) < (\mathbb{C}^*, \cdot)$.

**(iv)** $(\\{ 0, 2 \\}, +) < (\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$

**(v)** $(\\{ 0, 3 \\}, +) \not < (\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$ because $3 +3 = 2 \notin \\{ 0, 3\\}$.

**(vi)** $(\mathbb{Z}^+, \cdot) \not \le (\mathbb{Z}, +)$.

## Theorem
Let $H$ be a nonempty subset of a group $G$. Then 

$$H \le G \iff ab^{-1} \in H, \forall a, b \in H$$

## Proof
$(\Longrightarrow)$

Let $a, b \in H$. Since $H \le G$, it is closed under the binary operation on $G$ and every element in $H$ has its inverse in $H$. Thus, $b^{-1} \in H$ and so that $ab^{-1} \in H$. 

$(\Longleftarrow)$

Suppose that $ab^{-1} \in H, \forall a, b \in H$. Let $a, b \in H$. 

**(i)** Since $H \subset G$, $H$ is associative. 

**(ii)** Since $aa^{-1} = e \in H$, $H$ has an identity element. 

**(iii)** Since $ea^{-1} = a^{-1} \in H$, every element in $H$ has an inverse in $H$. 

**(iv)** Since $a(b^{-1})^{-1} = ab \in H$, $H$ is closed under the binary operation on $G$. 

Thus, $H \le G$. $\blacksquare$
